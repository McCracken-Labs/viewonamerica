How this is made, and how to read it

The Foreign Dispatch answers one question. What is the rest of the world actually saying about the United States? Not the version filtered through American commentary, but the words of each country's own press. Every morning it gathers top US-related headlines from around the world, translates them into English, and links straight to the original article.

There are two feeds. World Press is the daily front page. It carries mainstream and state outlets from a rotating set of countries. Independent collects nonprofit, reader-funded and investigative journalism, grouped by editorial orientation, for readers who want sharper analysis and a wider range of viewpoints.

How to read the leaning tags

No outlet is neutral, and pretending otherwise would defeat the purpose. On World Press, each source is tagged by its relationship to power.

Independent / neutral State-funded, broad editorial Pro-government State-controlled

Independent outlets are privately owned and editorially independent. State-funded, broad editorial outlets such as Al Jazeera and France 24 are publicly financed but carry a wide range of views. Pro-government outlets reliably follow their government's line without being formally controlled. State-controlled outlets such as RT and Global Times are direct instruments of state messaging. They are still worth reading, because they show what a government wants the world to believe.

The Independent page

Big outlets, even good ones, share blind spots. The Independent feed gathers the nonprofit newsrooms, reader-funded sites and investigative shops that tend to have more room for critical thinking. They are grouped by orientation, from investigative and independent-left to libertarian, conservative and heterodox, because reading across viewpoints beats sitting inside one. Independent media leans left worldwide, so right-of-centre and heterodox voices are included on purpose for balance.

One group deserves a special mention. Where authoritarian governments make independent journalism dangerous, newsrooms often move abroad and keep covering home, including its dealings with Washington. Outlets like Meduza, IranWire, Hong Kong Free Press and The Kyiv Independent carry US stories seen through the eyes of the people Washington affects most directly.

How sources rotate

To keep the site fresh and give a global picture over time, the World Press homepage shows a rotating slice of countries drawn from a larger pool. Whichever countries sit at the center of the day's biggest US story are always included. The rest rotate. Every edition is saved in the Archive.

An honest limitation

Some major Western outlets, including the BBC, The Guardian, Le Monde, Der Spiegel and the Times of India, block the automated tool this site uses to read the web. Where possible their absence is filled with other credible outlets from the same region. It does mean the accessible pool leans somewhat toward state-funded and Global South media. That is disclosed here rather than hidden.

A note on translation

Non-English headlines are translated to convey meaning and tone, not word for word. Where the original matters, it appears beneath the translation. For anything that carries weight, follow the link and read the source.
